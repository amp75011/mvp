{{ _('Thai Curry with Chickpeas, Pumpkin and Spinach') }}

{{ _('Ingredients for 2 Servings') }}

{{ _('• 1 cup cooked chickpeas (or canned)') }}

{{ _('• 300 g pumpkin (weighed before peeling)') }}

{{ _('• 1 small onion') }}

{{ _('• 1 teaspoon of red curry paste (or 1 teaspoon of grated garlic and ginger and 1/2 teaspoon of ground hot pepper)') }}

{{ _('• 1/2 teaspoon ground turmeric') }}

{{ _('• 150 ml coconut milk') }}

{{ _('• Approx. 1/2 cup of broth') }}

{{ _('• A large handful of spinach') }}

{{ _('• Coriander (for garnish)') }}

{{ _('• Fresh chili (for garnish)') }}

{{ _('• 1 lime wedge (for serving)') }}

{{ _('• Salt (to taste)') }}

{{ _('• 2 tablespoons of coconut oil (for frying)') }}

{{ _('• Rice (for serving)') }}

{{ _('Preparation') }}

{{ _('• Peel the pumpkin and cut into cubes. Cut the onion into slices.') }}

{{ _('• In a pan, fry the curry paste in 2 tablespoons of coconut milk. Add pumpkin, onion, season with salt and fry, stirring occasionally, for about 2 minutes.') }}

{{ _('• Add ground turmeric and cooked or canned chickpeas (if possible, add a few tablespoons of the chickpeas water). Add another 2 tablespoons of milk and fry for 1 minute.') }}

{{ _('• Pour in the rest of the coconut milk and bring to a boil. Cook for about 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Pour the broth gradually and cook for approx. 5 - 10 minutes until the pumpkin is soft. In the meantime, stir every now and then. Finally, add the spinach, mix and set aside from the heat.') }}

{{ _('• Sprinkle with coriander and fresh chili, serve with rice and a lime wedge.') }}

{{ _('Salmon Baked in Red Pesto') }}

{{ _('Ingredients for 2 Servings') }}

{{ _('• 600 g salmon') }}

{{ _('• Salt and pepper (to taste)') }}

{{ _('• 2 tablespoons of red pesto (made from dried tomatoes)') }}

{{ _('• 1/2 can of chopped, peeled tomatoes (or 1 fresh tomato)') }}

{{ _('• 2 teaspoons of dried oregano') }}

{{ _('• 2 tablespoons of extra olive oil') }}

{{ _('• Fresh basil (for garnish)') }}

{{ _('• 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese') }}

{{ _('Preparation') }}

{{ _('• Cut off the skin from the fillets, rinse the salmon, dry it and cut into portions. Season with salt and pepper and spread with red pesto. Place in an ovenproof dish.') }}

{{ _('• Place 2 tablespoons of chopped tomatoes on each portion and season with dried oregano, salt, and pepper. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with basil and Parmesan. Preheat the oven to 200 degrees C and bake for about 15 - 17 minutes.') }}

{{ _('Grilled Trout with Vegetables and Figs') }}

{{ _('Ingredients for 2 Servings') }}

{{ _('• 2 trout fillets') }}

{{ _('• 1 cup broccoli florets') }}

{{ _('• 1 cup green beans, ends trimmed') }}

{{ _('• 1 cup sliced carrots') }}

{{ _('• 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved') }}

{{ _('• 4 fresh figs, quartered') }}

{{ _('• 2 tablespoons olive oil') }}

{{ _('• Salt and pepper to taste') }}

{{ _('• Optional herbs like thyme or rosemary') }}

{{ _('Preparation') }}

{{ _('• Preheat your grill pan over medium-high heat. Brush the trout fillets with olive oil and season with salt, pepper, and optional herbs.') }}

{{ _('• Grill the trout for about 4-5 minutes per side or until it flakes easily with a fork.') }}

{{ _('• Steam broccoli, green beans, and carrots until just tender. Add them to the grill pan with the tomatoes, drizzle with olive oil, and season.') }}

{{ _('• Grill the vegetables for 2-3 minutes, then add the fig quarters and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.') }}

{{ _('• Serve the grilled trout and vegetables hot, with the figs adding a sweet contrast.') }}
